About

Built by one CS student.
For $10 a year.

I'm a computer science student in Singapore who got tired of waiting for the right moment to build something. Bharcode is my public experiment — can you build meaningful passive income with nothing but a domain name and free tools?

The answer so far: yes, but it takes compounding. This blog documents every step transparently — the wins, the flops, and the slow grind of small efforts adding up.

I started by launching a Hugo blog on GitHub Pages. Total cost: $10.35 for the domain. Everything else — hosting, email, analytics, design — runs on free tiers. From there, I'm building a network of affiliate posts, Pinterest pins, and automation bots that work while I study.

Why public? Accountability is free leverage. When you document everything, you ship more, cut corners less, and produce content as a natural side effect of working.
